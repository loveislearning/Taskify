✍️ Grammar Rules (Quick Reference)

  • Capitalize the first word of a sentence and proper nouns.
  • Use a semicolon, not a comma, before “hence” when it introduces an independent clause.
  • Spell out numbers one through nine, except in titles or when followed by a unit.
  • Use “a number of” with a plural verb: “A number of points are...”
  • Capitalize nouns followed by numerals: “Experiment 2,” “Table 6.”
  • Use “fewer” for countable nouns, “less” for uncountable nouns.
  • Place punctuation inside quotation marks in American English.
  • Use “who” for people, “which/that” for things.
  • Use the Oxford comma in lists for clarity.
  • “Its” is possessive; “it’s” means “it is.”
  • Use “affect” as a verb, “effect” as a noun (usually).
  • Use “whom” when it’s the object of a verb or preposition.
  • “Lie” (to recline) vs. “Lay” (to put down).
  • Use “between” for two, “among” for more than two.
  • “Farther” for physical distance, “further” for figurative distance.
  • Use “may” for permission, “can” for ability.

⚖️ Fundamental Physics Laws

  • Newton’s First Law: An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force.
  • Newton’s Second Law: \( F = m \cdot a \)
  • Newton’s Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
  • Law of Universal Gravitation: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)
  • Ohm’s Law: \( V = I \cdot R \)
  • Faraday’s Law of Induction: The induced EMF in a circuit is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux.
  • Hooke’s Law: \( F = -kx \)
  • Boyle’s Law: \( P_1V_1 = P_2V_2 \) (at constant T)
  • Charles’s Law: \( V_1/T_1 = V_2/T_2 \) (at constant P)
  • First Law of Thermodynamics: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
  • Second Law of Thermodynamics: Entropy of an isolated system always increases.
  • Coulomb’s Law: \( F = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
